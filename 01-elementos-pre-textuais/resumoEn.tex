\begin{resumo}[Abstract]

This work develops the architecture and firmware of a passive UHF RFID reader prototype for asset inventory, based on a YPD-R200 module, a linearly polarized 5~dBi Airplux APCA8090 antenna, AD-239 M730 tags, and an Arduino. The proposal replaces an initial NFC-based design whose short operating range still requires items to be checked individually. The reader library was adapted to the Arduino Uno/Nano TTL UART interface through the \texttt{Stream} abstraction and \texttt{SoftwareSerial}, supporting output-power commands and variable-length EPCs. The physical experimental campaign originally planned was replaced by a stochastic scenario simulation because of schedule and budget constraints, the cost of additional antennas, tags, instrumentation, and positioning fixtures, and the lack of access to a controlled environment. The synthetic dataset combines logarithmic path loss, geometry, orientation, attachment material, tag-to-tag differences, multipath, fading, and Bernoulli/binomial sampling; it is neither a full-wave electromagnetic simulation nor a measurement of the prototype. In the generated scenarios, the mean simulated read rate was 96.9\% at 0.5~m, 95.1\% at 1.0~m, 93.1\% at 1.5~m, and 90.0\% at 2.0~m. Simulated rates at $0^\circ$, $45^\circ$, and $90^\circ$ were 89.1\%, 53.8\%, and 10.0\%, respectively. The model assigned 3.3\% detection to direct attachment on metal and 30.0\% to a 10~mm spacer. In the simulated ten-tag room inventory, mean coverage was 91.0\%, corresponding to a 9.0\% omission rate, but only three of ten windows were complete. These outputs support trend exploration and metric verification but do not validate the physical range or reliability of the equipment. For portable scanning, an omnidirectional radiation pattern is a potential alternative for broader azimuthal coverage; however, antennas were not compared in the simulation, and circular or dual polarization remains the more direct alternative for reducing orientation sensitivity. The project therefore provides firmware foundations, an exploratory model, and a protocol for future physical validation, which should compare antenna configurations under controlled conditions.

\textbf{Keywords:} UHF RFID. Asset inventory. YPD-R200. Scenario simulation. RFID antennas.

\end{resumo}
